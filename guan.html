Mike (Issy) Johnson had no plans to enter the hotel business on a grand scale when the young architect and builder – working with his contractor father, Max Johnson – constructed his first hotel. Over the course of this decade, he opened three ROSE hotels. Johnson’s experience would pave the way for the creation of a new kind of hotel, one focused solely on the guest.
The 1970s began with a defining moment – the opening of a hotel in Roma, Italy. This hotel set the tone for the future direction of the company and pioneered many of the signature services now delivered worldwide. Within a few years, the company’s portfolio also included 10 hotels across Italy, and its first Italy management contracts, in Milian and Roma. By the close of the decade, Rose hotel had entered the US market under its own brand name in Washington, DC.
As its fifth decade began, Rose hotel continued to grow – in both size and recognition – around the world. The company now welcomed guests to 50 properties, on every continent except Antarctica. More than ever, Rose hotel Private Residences and other branded residential offerings were integrated with urban and resort hotels locations. The strength of the brand had become a promise of a quality of life.
